# T01 — Economic Integration: The 6 Stages

**Spanish label:** Otros bloques (Unión Europea, Mercosur)
**Appears in lectures:** L16
**Textbook:** Hill Ch. 9 (verify edition)
**Exam priority:** HIGH — teacher flagged this as certain to appear on partial exam 2

## 1. TL;DR
Regional economic integration is the progressive removal of trade and factor barriers among a group of countries. There are **six stages**, each building cumulatively on the previous one: Free Trade Area → Customs Union → Common Market → Economic Union → Monetary Union → Political/Complete Union. Each successive stage adds one defining element (common external tariff, free movement of factors, policy coordination, common currency, central government) on top of all prior stages.

## 2. The 6 stages (in order)

### 1. Free Trade Area (FTA)
- **What changes:** Barriers to trade between member countries are eliminated. Each member **keeps its own tariffs** against non-members. Because external tariffs differ, **rules of origin** are required to stop third-country goods being triangulated in via the lowest-tariff member.
- **Example(s):** NAFTA / USMCA / TMEC (Mexico–US–Canada).

### 2. Customs Union
- **What changes:** FTA **+** a **harmonized external tariff** against non-member (third) countries. Because all members charge the same external tariff, rules of origin are no longer needed internally.
- **Example(s):** Few pure examples remain today; most have advanced to common market. Historically the Andean Community.

### 3. Common Market
- **What changes:** Customs Union **+** free movement of **factors of production** (labor and capital), not just goods and services.
- **Example(s):** **Mercosur** (Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay, with associates) — in practice bureaucratic barriers to labor mobility persist.

### 4. Economic Union
- **What changes:** Common Market **+** coordination of core economic policies — specifically **exchange rate, inflation, and fiscal deficit**.
- **Example(s):** **Non-Eurozone EU members** — Hungary, Poland, Sweden, Denmark, Czech Republic, Romania, Bulgaria.

### 5. Monetary Union
- **What changes:** Economic Union **+** a **common currency** (and a single monetary authority).
- **Example(s):** The **Eurozone**.

### 6. Political / Complete Union
- **What changes:** Integration into a **single country with a central government**. A complete union ideally also harmonizes fiscal policy (a "fiscal union").
- **Example(s):** The **United States** (50 states), **Mexico** (32 states — a complete *fiscal* union too, with uniform income tax across states).
- **Watch out:** The **EU is often criticized for being an incomplete union** — it has a common currency (for the Eurozone) but is **not a full fiscal union**: member states set their own tax rates. The US is also not a full fiscal union (states have different income taxes; some have none). Mexico, uniquely among the three, is.

## 3. Key terminology
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Free Trade Area (FTA) | Bloc where internal trade barriers are removed but each member keeps its own external tariffs. |
| Customs Union | FTA plus a common external tariff against non-members. |
| Common Market | Customs union plus free movement of factors of production. |
| Economic Union | Common market plus coordination of exchange rate, inflation, and fiscal deficit. |
| Monetary Union | Economic union plus a common currency. |
| Political / Complete Union | A single country with a central government. |
| Rules of origin | Criteria determining a good's country of origin; required in an FTA to prevent triangulation of third-country goods. |
| Factors of production | Inputs to production — principally **labor and capital** — that move freely from the Common Market stage onward. |
| Fiscal Union | A union in which tax rates and tax policy are harmonized across members (Mexico yes; EU and US no). |
| Mercosur | South American common market — standard teacher example of stage 3. |
| Eurozone | Subset of EU members sharing the euro — standard teacher example of stage 5. |

## 6. Watch out / uncertainties
- **Stages are cumulative** — each level *includes everything from lower levels*. When asked to "describe" a stage, state the defining addition **and** note it carries forward all prior properties.
- **Rules of origin belong to the FTA stage** and disappear once members adopt a common external tariff (Customs Union onward). Do not attach rules of origin to Customs Union.
- **Mercosur = Common Market** (teacher's canonical example), not a customs union, even though in practice labor mobility is imperfect.
- **Non-euro EU members = Economic Union**, *not* Monetary Union — the common currency is the dividing line.
- **Mexico is a complete fiscal union** (32 states, uniform income tax); the **US and EU are not**. Useful one-liner for the "complete union" stage.
